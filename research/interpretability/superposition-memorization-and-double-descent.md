## One-Liner

Investigates how neural networks overfit by storing data points (not features) in superposition, observing double descent as models transition between regimes.

## Key Points

- Overfitting corresponds to storing data points in superposition, rather than features
- Models fall into two regimes: overfitting (storing data points) and generalizing (storing features)
- Observes double descent as the model transitions between these regimes
- Superposition may be key to understanding memorization in language models
