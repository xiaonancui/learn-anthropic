## One-Liner

Investigates how neural networks represent more features than they have dimensions (superposition) using small ReLU networks trained on synthetic sparse data.

## Key Points

- Superposition: models represent more features than they have dimensions when features are sparse
- Allows compression beyond what linear models can do
- Comes at the cost of "interference" requiring nonlinear filtering
- Uses toy models (small ReLU networks) with synthetic data to study the phenomenon
