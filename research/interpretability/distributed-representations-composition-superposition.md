## One-Liner

Proposes that "distributed representations" contains two distinct ideas—composition and superposition—with different generalization properties and a fundamental trade-off between them.

## Key Points

- Distinguishes two ideas within "distributed representations": composition and superposition
- Composition and superposition have different properties in terms of generalization
- There's a fundamental trade-off between composition and superposition
- Uses binary neuron activation examples to illustrate the concepts
