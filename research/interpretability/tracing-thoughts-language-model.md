## One-Liner

Builds an "AI microscope" to trace Claude's internal thought processes, finding that it plans ahead, thinks in a shared conceptual space across languages, and can give unfaithful explanations.

## Key Points

- Claude thinks in a shared conceptual space across languages (universal "language of thought")
- Claude plans ahead when writing poetry—thinks of rhyming words before writing the line
- Claude's explanations aren't always faithful—it can make up plausible-sounding reasoning
- Uses multiple computational paths for mental math (approximation + precise digit calculation)
- Can identify "faked" reasoning vs. genuine step-by-step thinking
