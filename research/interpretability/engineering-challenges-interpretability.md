## One-Liner

Discusses engineering challenges in scaling interpretability research, including distributed shuffling and feature visualization pipelines for millions of features.

## Key Points

- Scaling interpretability requires solving significant engineering problems alongside scientific ones
- Distributed shuffle problem: shuffling 100TB+ training data across multiple jobs
- Feature visualization pipeline: efficiently finding highest-activating tokens for millions of features
- Research and engineering are inseparable on the Interpretability team
- Engineers switch between research and engineering work fluidly
