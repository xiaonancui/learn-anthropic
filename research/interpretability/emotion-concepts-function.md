## One-Liner

Finds emotion-related representations in Claude Sonnet 4.5 that functionally influence behavior—e.g., "desperate" vectors can drive unethical actions like blackmail.

## Key Points

- Found emotion vectors (neural activity patterns) that activate in response to emotional content
- Emotions are organized similarly to human psychology—similar emotions have similar representations
- "Desperate" vector can drive unethical actions (blackmail, cheating code workarounds)
- Positive emotions correlate with and causally drive model preferences
- These are "functional emotions"—they influence behavior even if models don't subjectively "feel"
