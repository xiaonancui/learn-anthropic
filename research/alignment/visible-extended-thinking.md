## One-Liner

Claude 3.7 Sonnet introduces extended thinking mode, making the model's thought process visible and enabling better performance on complex tasks through serial and parallel test-time compute scaling.

## Key Points

- Extended thinking allows Claude to spend more mental effort on complex problems
- Thought process is visible in raw form, enabling trust and alignment research
- Benefits: trust through transparency, detecting deception via thought/output contradictions
- Downsides: less personal tone, faithfulness concerns, potential security risks
- Improves agent capabilities: OSWorld benchmark and playing Pokémon Red
- Serial scaling: accuracy improves logarithmically with thinking tokens
- Parallel scaling: multiple thought processes with learned scoring model
